🏆 Prompt of the Month
The Decision Matrix Builder

Stuck choosing between options? Whether it's picking a supplier, selecting a software tool, or deciding between design approaches, this prompt builds a weighted scoring matrix for you in seconds.

You are a business analyst helping me make a structured decision. I need to choose between [NUMBER] options for [DESCRIBE THE DECISION e.g. selecting a new ERP system, choosing a PCB supplier, picking a trade show to attend]. The options are: 1. [OPTION 1] 2. [OPTION 2] 3. [OPTION 3] Please: 1. Suggest 5-6 relevant evaluation criteria for this type of decision 2. Assign a weight (1-5) to each criterion based on typical importance 3. Score each option against each criterion (1-10) 4. Calculate weighted totals and rank the options 5. Summarise which option wins and why in 2-3 sentences Format the scoring as a clear table.

What you'll get back: A professional weighted decision matrix with criteria, scores, totals, and a clear recommendation — perfect for board papers, procurement decisions, or any time you need to justify a choice.

💡 Why this works: This uses step-by-step instructions (numbered 1-5) so ChatGPT works through the problem methodically rather than jumping straight to an answer. It also requests a specific output format (table), which means less reformatting for you.

📈 1. Sales & Marketing
Customer Objection Response Prep
Act as a senior sales engineer at Harwin, a UK manufacturer of high-reliability connectors. A prospect has raised this objection: "[OBJECTION e.g. Your connectors cost more than the competition, We've always used our current supplier, Lead times are too long]." Write 3 different responses I could use: 1. A short, confident response for a live call (2-3 sentences) 2. A detailed email response with supporting evidence (under 150 words) 3. A one-line reframe that turns the objection into a selling point For each, reference Harwin's strengths in [RELEVANT AREA e.g. reliability testing, AS9100 certification, UK-based manufacturing].
💡 Why this works: Asking for three different formats of the same answer gives you options for different situations — phone, email, and quick reframe — all from one prompt.
Trade Show Stand Talking Points
You are preparing the Harwin sales team for [TRADE SHOW e.g. Embedded World, DSEI, Space Tech Expo]. Create a one-page cheat sheet with: 1. A 30-second elevator pitch for Harwin (for visitors who say "So what do you do?") 2. 3 opening questions to qualify visitors quickly 3. Key talking points for our [PRODUCT FAMILY e.g. Gecko, M300, Datamate] range 4. 2 competitor differentiators we should mention 5. A smooth close to book a follow-up meeting Keep it conversational — this is for real conversations, not a brochure.
💡 Why this works: The numbered structure creates a practical cheat sheet your team can print out and carry to the stand. "Keep it conversational" stops ChatGPT writing stiff marketing copy.
Quarterly Sales Email to Dormant Accounts
Act as a Harwin account manager. Write a re-engagement email to a customer we haven't heard from in [TIMEFRAME e.g. 6 months, 1 year]. Their last order was for [PRODUCT e.g. Datamate connectors for a defence project]. The email should: - Feel personal, not automated - Reference something relevant in their industry (e.g. a recent regulation change, trade show, or technology trend) - Mention one new Harwin product or capability that might interest them - End with a soft call to action (not pushy) Keep it under 120 words.
💡 Why this works: Asking ChatGPT to "reference something relevant in their industry" forces it to think about context, making the email feel researched rather than mass-mailed.
⚙️ 2. Engineering / R&D
Material Selection Trade-Off Analysis
You are a materials engineer specialising in electronic connectors. I need to choose between [MATERIAL A e.g. phosphor bronze] and [MATERIAL B e.g. beryllium copper] for the contact elements in a [CONNECTOR TYPE e.g. 1.25mm-pitch board-to-board connector] used in [APPLICATION e.g. satellite communications]. Compare them across these criteria: - Electrical conductivity - Spring-back / fatigue resistance - Operating temperature range - Corrosion resistance - Cost and availability - Compliance considerations (RoHS, REACH) Present as a comparison table, then give a 3-sentence recommendation explaining which you'd choose and why.
💡 Why this works: Listing the exact criteria you care about stops ChatGPT from giving you an irrelevant essay. The table + recommendation structure gives you both data and a conclusion.
Test Report Executive Summary
I'm going to paste the results from a [TEST TYPE e.g. vibration, thermal cycling, salt spray] test on [PRODUCT e.g. Gecko SL connector]. Write an executive summary (5-6 sentences) that a non-technical manager could understand. Include: what was tested, the pass/fail criteria, the result, and any notable observations. End with a one-sentence recommendation for next steps. [PASTE YOUR TEST DATA OR NOTES HERE]
💡 Why this works: Specifying "a non-technical manager" as the audience automatically adjusts the language. The "recommendation for next steps" makes the summary actionable, not just informational.
Patent Search Preparation Brief
Act as a patent research assistant. I'm developing a [DESCRIBE INVENTION e.g. new locking mechanism for a high-density PCB connector]. Before I approach the patent team, help me prepare: 1. Suggest 10 key search terms and phrases I should use in a patent database 2. Identify 3-4 patent classification codes (IPC/CPC) most likely relevant 3. List the types of prior art I should look for (not just patents — think papers, products, standards) 4. Draft a 1-paragraph invention summary I can give to the patent attorney Note: this is for research preparation only, not legal advice.
💡 Why this works: The disclaimer "for research preparation only, not legal advice" is a good habit — it reminds you (and ChatGPT) of the boundary of what AI should and shouldn't do.
💰 3. Finance / Accounts
Month-End Commentary Generator
You are a management accountant at a UK electronics manufacturer. Write month-end commentary for [MONTH/YEAR] using the following data: - Revenue: £[ACTUAL] vs budget £[BUDGET] - Gross margin: [ACTUAL]% vs budget [BUDGET]% - Key cost variance: [DESCRIBE e.g. raw materials up 8% due to copper price rises] - Headcount: [ACTUAL] vs budget [BUDGET] - Cash position: £[AMOUNT] Write 2 short paragraphs: one covering revenue and margin performance, one covering costs and cash. Use a professional tone suitable for a board pack. Avoid adjectives like "impressive" or "disappointing" — stick to factual language.
💡 Why this works: The instruction to "avoid adjectives like impressive or disappointing" stops ChatGPT from editorialising. Finance commentary should let the numbers do the talking.
FX Impact Explanation
Explain the following FX impact in simple terms for a non-finance audience: - We invoiced [CURRENCY e.g. USD] [AMOUNT] at a rate of [RATE] - Budget rate was [BUDGET RATE] - This created a [FAVOURABLE/ADVERSE] variance of £[AMOUNT] Write 3-4 sentences explaining what happened, why it matters, and whether this is a one-off or a trend. Use an analogy if it helps. This is for a presentation to department heads who don't work in finance.
💡 Why this works: Asking for an analogy is a brilliant way to make financial concepts land with non-finance colleagues. ChatGPT is surprisingly good at this.
Travel Expense Policy Q&A

Based on this policy, answer the following question clearly and concisely: "[QUESTION e.g. Can I claim for a business dinner with a supplier if I'm not staying overnight? What's the limit?]" Quote the specific section of the policy that applies. If the policy doesn't clearly cover this scenario, say so and suggest what clarification might be needed. [PASTE POLICY TEXT HERE]
💡 Why this works: Asking ChatGPT to "quote the specific section" keeps it grounded in the source material instead of making up rules. The fallback instruction ("if the policy doesn't cover this, say so") prevents hallucination.
🚚 4. Operations / Supply Chain
Supplier Scorecard Builder
Act as a procurement manager at an electronics connector manufacturer. Create a supplier performance scorecard for [SUPPLIER NAME] based on the following data: - On-time delivery rate: [PERCENTAGE]% - Quality reject rate: [PERCENTAGE]% - Price competitiveness: [RATING e.g. competitive, above market, below market] - Responsiveness: [DESCRIPTION e.g. replies within 24 hours, often delayed] - Number of NCRs in the last 12 months: [NUMBER] Rate each criterion as Green/Amber/Red with a short justification. Add an overall recommendation: Continue, Monitor, or Escalate. Format as a professional scorecard table.
💡 Why this works: The traffic-light rating (Green/Amber/Red) is a universal business language. It instantly communicates status without requiring explanation.
Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys)
You are a lean manufacturing specialist. Help me run a 5 Whys root cause analysis on this problem: Problem: [DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM e.g. Line 3 had 2 hours unplanned downtime on Tuesday due to a tooling jam] For each "Why", suggest the most likely answer based on common manufacturing issues, then ask the next "Why." After the 5th Why, suggest: 1. The root cause 2. A short-term containment action 3. A long-term corrective action Format clearly with numbered Whys.
💡 Why this works: The 5 Whys technique is a proven lean methodology. Getting ChatGPT to walk through it step by step gives you a structured analysis you can paste into a CAPA report.
Shift Handover Note
I'm going to paste my rough notes from today's shift. Please rewrite them as a clear, professional shift handover note with these sections: - Completed: what was finished today - In Progress: what's running and its status - Issues: any problems encountered and current status - Handover Actions: specific things the next shift needs to do Keep it factual and concise. Use bullet points under each section. [PASTE YOUR ROUGH NOTES HERE]
💡 Why this works: Turning rough notes into structured handover documents is one of the highest-ROI uses of ChatGPT in operations. You save 15 minutes and the next shift gets clear information.
👥 5. HR / People
Performance Review Sentence Starter
Act as an HR professional. I need to write performance review comments for an employee. Here's the context: - Role: [JOB TITLE] - Key achievement: [DESCRIBE e.g. reduced scrap rate by 12% through a new inspection process] - Area for development: [DESCRIBE e.g. needs to improve delegation and trust the team more] - Overall rating: [e.g. Meets expectations, Exceeds expectations] Write: 1. A 3-sentence summary of their performance this period 2. Two specific strengths with examples 3. One development area framed constructively (not negatively) 4. A suggested development goal for the next review period Use professional language that's supportive and motivating.
💡 Why this works: "Framed constructively (not negatively)" is a key instruction. ChatGPT will write "Sarah could benefit from delegating more" instead of "Sara fails to delegate" — tone control at its best.
New Starter Onboarding Checklist
Create a first-week onboarding checklist for a new [JOB TITLE] joining the [DEPARTMENT] team at Harwin, a UK electronics manufacturer. Include: - Pre-arrival tasks (IT setup, desk, etc.) - Day 1 activities (welcome, tour, key introductions) - Week 1 learning goals and key meetings to attend - Key systems they need access to: [LIST e.g. SAP, SharePoint, Power BI] - A buddy or go-to person structure Format as a checklist with tick boxes (use checkbox characters). Group by day/timeframe.
💡 Why this works: "Group by day/timeframe" creates a timeline-based checklist instead of a flat list. This makes it practical for both HR and the new starter's manager to follow.
Difficult Conversation Script
Act as an experienced HR business partner. Help me prepare for a difficult conversation with an employee about [TOPIC e.g. persistent lateness, underperformance, attitude concerns]. Write: 1. An opening statement (how to start the conversation — 2-3 sentences) 2. 3 key points I need to cover, with suggested phrasing 3. 2 likely pushbacks the employee might give, with suggested responses 4. A closing statement that's firm but fair Use language that is direct, empathetic, and legally safe for a UK workplace. Avoid accusatory phrasing.
💡 Why this works: "Legally safe for a UK workplace" gives ChatGPT jurisdiction context. UK employment law is different from US law, and this instruction helps keep the language appropriate.
📊 6. Data & Analytics
DAX Measure Builder
You are a Power BI developer. I need a DAX measure that calculates [DESCRIBE WHAT YOU NEED e.g. "year-over-year growth percentage for revenue, handling cases where last year's value is zero"]. My data model has these relevant tables and columns: - [TABLE1]: [COLUMN1, COLUMN2, ...] - [TABLE2]: [COLUMN1, COLUMN2, ...] - Date table: Date, Year, Month, Quarter Write the DAX measure with: 1. The complete measure formula 2. A line-by-line explanation of what each part does 3. One common mistake to avoid when using this measure 4. A suggested name following best practices (e.g. "Revenue YoY %")
💡 Why this works: Including your actual table and column names means the DAX formula works immediately in your model. The "common mistake" section saves you debugging time.
Data Cleaning Steps Generator
I have a messy dataset that I need to clean before analysis. Here's what I know about it: - Source: [e.g. Excel export from SAP, CSV from a supplier portal] - Rows: approximately [NUMBER] - Known issues: [LIST e.g. duplicate customer names with different spellings, dates in mixed formats, blank fields in the region column, negative values in the quantity column] Create a step-by-step data cleaning plan with: 1. The order I should tackle the issues (most impactful first) 2. For each step, the specific action and a sample [TOOL e.g. Excel formula, Power Query step, Python/pandas code] 3. A validation check after each step to confirm it worked Format as a numbered checklist.
💡 Why this works: The "validation check after each step" instruction is gold — it turns a plan into a testable workflow. You know exactly when something's gone wrong.
Stakeholder Requirements to Report Spec
I've been asked to build a report. Here's what the stakeholder said (their exact words): "[PASTE THEIR REQUEST e.g. I need to see how our bookings are trending versus last year, broken down by region, and I want to spot which product families are growing]" Translate this into a structured report specification with: 1. Report title suggestion 2. Key metrics / KPIs to include (with calculation definitions) 3. Dimensions / filters needed 4. Suggested visualisation types for each metric 5. Refresh frequency recommendation 6. Questions I should go back and ask the stakeholder before building
💡 Why this works: The final section — "questions I should go back and ask" — is the secret weapon. ChatGPT identifies the gaps in the brief so you don't build the wrong thing.
✅ 7. Quality / Compliance
CAPA (Corrective Action) Draft
Act as a quality engineer at an aerospace connector manufacturer (AS9100D certified). Draft a Corrective Action report for the following issue: - NCR/CAPA number: [NUMBER] - Problem description: [DESCRIBE e.g. 3 out of 500 connectors failed contact resistance testing at incoming inspection] - Root cause identified: [DESCRIBE e.g. plating bath chemistry out of specification during third shift] Write sections for: 1. Problem Statement (3 sentences, factual) 2. Root Cause Analysis (reference the 5 Whys or fishbone as appropriate) 3. Containment Action (immediate steps taken) 4. Corrective Action (permanent fix) 5. Verification Plan (how we'll confirm the fix works) 6. Preventive Action (stop it happening elsewhere) Keep each section to 2-4 sentences. Use formal quality language.
💡 Why this works: The six-section structure mirrors a real CAPA form. ChatGPT gives you a professionally structured first draft that you then refine with your specific knowledge.
Regulation Change Impact Summary
Summarise the impact of [REGULATION CHANGE e.g. the updated EU RoHS exemption list, new REACH SVHC additions, updated IPC-A-610 revision] on a UK-based electronics connector manufacturer. Structure your response as: 1. What changed (2-3 sentences, plain English) 2. Who it affects at Harwin (which departments/processes) 3. What we need to do (specific actions, prioritised) 4. Deadline or timeline 5. Risk if we do nothing Important: flag if any of your information might be outdated and suggest I verify with [RELEVANT BODY e.g. HSE, BSI, ECHA].
💡 Why this works: The "flag if information might be outdated" instruction adds a built-in safety net. For compliance work, knowing what ChatGPT is unsure about is just as valuable as what it knows.
Internal Audit Finding Write-Up
I conducted an internal audit and found the following issue: - Area audited: [e.g. goods-in inspection, document control, calibration] - Standard clause: [e.g. AS9100D clause 8.5.1, ISO 9001 clause 7.1.5] - What I observed: [DESCRIBE e.g. 3 out of 10 calibration certificates were expired] - Classification: [Minor / Major / Observation] Write a formal audit finding that includes: 1. Finding statement (factual, objective, referencing the clause) 2. Objective evidence (what I observed, stated clearly) 3. Requirement (what the standard says should happen) 4. Suggested corrective action timeframe Write in third person, formal audit language.
💡 Why this works: "Third person, formal audit language" is a precise style instruction. Audit findings that read like personal opinions get challenged; ones that read like professional observations get actioned.

Rewrite it as a step-by-step user guide for non-technical staff. Requirements: - Use numbered steps with simple language - Include "what you'll see" descriptions for each screen - Add a "Troubleshooting" section for the 3 most common issues - Start with a "Before you begin" checklist - Keep it under 1 page when printed [PASTE TECHNICAL DOCS HERE]

Phishing Email Awareness Quiz
Create a short phishing awareness quiz for employees at a UK manufacturing company. Write 5 realistic email scenarios — some legitimate, some phishing. For each: 1. Show the email (From, Subject, Body — keep it realistic and relevant to our industry) 2. Ask: "Phishing or legitimate? What clues gave it away?" 3. Provide the answer with an explanation of the red flags Make the phishing examples convincing (not obvious) and relevant to [INDUSTRY CONTEXT e.g. electronics manufacturing, supplier communications, delivery notifications]. End with 3 quick tips for spotting phishing attempts.
💡 Why this works: "Make the phishing examples convincing, not obvious" is the critical instruction here. Easy-to-spot phishing doesn't train anyone — realistic examples build real skills.
✅ Do's and Don'ts — Getting Better Results
Do
✅ Give ChatGPT context about your audience. "Write this for a board meeting" produces very different output from "Write this for a team stand-up." Always say who's going to read it.
✅ Use "Think step by step" for complex problems. Adding this phrase to analysis or decision-making prompts forces ChatGPT to show its reasoning rather than jumping to conclusions.
✅ Provide examples of what good looks like. If you want a specific tone or format, paste a sample and say "Write in this style." ChatGPT mimics examples beautifully.
✅ Break big tasks into smaller steps. Instead of "Write me a business case," try "First, outline the problem. Then write the proposed solution. Then list the costs and benefits." Sequential instructions give sequential output.
✅ Save your best prompts. When a prompt works well, save it somewhere. Building a personal prompt library means you're getting faster every month, not starting from scratch.
Don't
❌ Don't accept the first response as final. The first answer is a draft. Say "Make it shorter," "Add more detail on the risk," or "Rewrite in a more formal tone." The best results come from iteration.
❌ Don't forget to set the tone. Without a tone instruction, ChatGPT defaults to generic professional. Add "Use a warm, approachable tone" or "Keep it formal and concise" for much better results.
❌ Don't paste entire spreadsheets without guidance. ChatGPT can handle data, but it needs to know what you want. "Here's a spreadsheet — analyse it" is too vague. "Calculate the monthly trend and flag any anomalies" is much better.
❌ Don't use AI output for safety-critical decisions without expert review. ChatGPT is excellent for drafting, brainstorming, and structuring — but engineering specifications, regulatory declarations, and safety assessments must always be verified by qualified professionals.
❌ Don't assume ChatGPT remembers previous conversations. Each new chat starts fresh. If you need context from a previous session, paste the key information in again.
💡 Tips & Tricks
Tip #1: The "Before and After" technique. Paste your rough draft and ask "Improve this while keeping the same meaning and length." You'll get a polished version in seconds. This works brilliantly for emails, reports, and internal comms — it's like having an editor on call.
Tip #2: Use "What questions should I be asking?" When you're starting a new project or investigation, ask ChatGPT this before diving in. It'll often surface angles you hadn't considered. Think of it as a brainstorming partner that doesn't get tired.
Tip #3: The "Explain it to a 12-year-old" test. If ChatGPT gives you something too complex, say "Explain this as if I'm 12 years old." This strips away jargon and forces clarity. It's also a great way to check whether you truly understand a concept yourself.
🏭 Real Harwin Use Case
This month: Operations / Supply Chain

A member of the Operations team needed to write a response to a customer asking why their order was delayed. The delay was caused by a raw material shortage from a tier-2 supplier, but explaining the full supply chain issue without alarming the customer was tricky. They used this prompt:

Act as a professional operations coordinator at Harwin. A customer has asked why their order for Datamate connectors is 2 weeks behind schedule. The real reason is a raw material delivery delay from our gold plating supplier. Write a transparent but reassuring email that explains the situation without oversharing internal details. Include a revised timeline and a gesture of goodwill. Keep it under 120 words.

The result was a professional, empathetic email that acknowledged the delay, gave a credible explanation ("a temporary disruption in our specialist materials supply chain"), provided a firm revised date, and offered priority shipping at no extra cost. The customer replied positively within an hour. Total time from blank screen to sent email: 4 minutes.
