🏆 Prompt of the Month
The Universal Summariser

This one works for literally anyone — Sales, Engineering, Finance, you name it. Paste in any long text and get a clean, structured summary in seconds.

You are a professional summariser. I'm going to paste a long document below. Please: 1. Give me a 2-sentence summary of the main point 2. List the 5 most important takeaways as bullet points 3. Flag anything that requires action from me Here is the document: [PASTE YOUR TEXT HERE]

What you'll get back: A short summary paragraph, five bullet points of key info, and a clear list of any action items — perfect for long emails, reports, or meeting notes.

💡 Why this works: It uses two powerful techniques: giving ChatGPT a role ("You are a professional summariser") so it knows what standard to aim for, and numbering your requests (1, 2, 3) so you get structured output instead of a wall of text.

📈 1. Sales & Marketing
Follow-Up Email After a Meeting or Trade Show
Act as a professional sales representative for Harwin, a UK-based manufacturer of high-reliability connectors and PCB hardware. Write a friendly follow-up email to a potential customer I met at [TRADE SHOW OR MEETING NAME]. They were interested in our [PRODUCT FAMILY e.g. Gecko, Datamate, Kona] connectors for their [APPLICATION e.g. defence avionics, medical devices, satellite systems]. Keep it under 150 words, professional but warm, and include a clear call to action to book a 15-minute call.
💡 Why this works: Giving ChatGPT your company name, product names, and the customer's industry means the email sounds like it came from someone at Harwin — not a generic template.
Competitor Product Comparison Table
You are a market research analyst for a precision connector company. Create a comparison table with these columns: Feature, Harwin [PRODUCT FAMILY e.g. Gecko], and [COMPETITOR PRODUCT]. Include rows for: contact density, operating temperature range, vibration resistance, weight, and typical application. Use publicly available specification data only. Add a row at the bottom for "Key differentiator" summarising each product's main advantage.
💡 Why this works: Asking for a specific table format with named columns and rows gives you structured, comparable data instead of a wall of text.
LinkedIn Post About a Product Launch
Write a short LinkedIn post (under 100 words) announcing that Harwin has launched [NEW PRODUCT OR UPDATE]. The tone should be professional but enthusiastic. Mention the key benefit for [TARGET MARKET e.g. aerospace engineers, defence OEMs, medical device designers]. End with a question to encourage engagement. Include 3-4 relevant hashtags.
💡 Why this works: Setting a word limit ("under 100 words") stops ChatGPT rambling. Asking it to "end with a question" gives you engagement-ready content.
⚙️ 2. Engineering / R&D
Design Review Checklist
You are an experienced electronics hardware engineer. Create a design review checklist for a [TYPE OF PRODUCT e.g. 2mm-pitch PCB connector, cable assembly] intended for [APPLICATION e.g. space-grade, military ground vehicle, medical implant]. Include sections for: mechanical fit, electrical performance, environmental testing, material compliance, and manufacturability. Format as a numbered checklist I can print out.
💡 Why this works: "You are an experienced electronics hardware engineer" makes ChatGPT draw on technical knowledge rather than giving a generic list.
Explain a Specification in Plain English
Explain the following technical specification in plain English that a non-engineer (e.g. a sales representative or product manager) could understand. Use an everyday analogy if possible. Keep it to 3-4 sentences. [PASTE THE SPECIFICATION TEXT HERE]
💡 Why this works: Telling ChatGPT who the audience is ("a non-engineer") controls the level of detail. Great for bridging Engineering and Sales.
Failure Mode Brainstorm
Act as a reliability engineer. I'm designing a [CONNECTOR TYPE] for use in [ENVIRONMENT e.g. high-vibration aerospace, extreme temperature space]. List 10 potential failure modes I should consider, organised by category (mechanical, electrical, environmental, material). For each, suggest one mitigation approach. Format as a table with columns: Category, Failure Mode, Mitigation.
💡 Why this works: Requesting a specific table format with named columns means the output goes straight into an FMEA worksheet.
💰 3. Finance / Accounts
Budget Variance Narrative
You are a management accountant. Write a short paragraph (4-5 sentences) explaining the following budget variance for a board pack: Budget: £[AMOUNT] Actual: £[AMOUNT] Variance: £[AMOUNT] ([PERCENTAGE]%) Main reason: [BRIEF REASON e.g. raw material price increase, FX movement, unplanned overtime] Keep the tone factual, professional, and jargon-free.
💡 Why this works: Providing specific data points upfront means ChatGPT writes the narrative around your actual numbers instead of guessing.
Polite Invoice Chase Email
Write a polite but firm email chasing an overdue invoice. Details: - Invoice number: [NUMBER] - Amount: £[AMOUNT] - Due date: [DATE] - Customer: [COMPANY NAME] Keep it under 100 words. Maintain a positive business relationship tone. Include the next step if payment isn't received within 7 days.
💡 Why this works: Feeding details as bullet points is one of the easiest ways to give ChatGPT structured information. It weaves them into natural prose automatically.
Explain a Financial Term Simply
Explain [FINANCIAL TERM e.g. EBITDA, working capital, gross margin] in 2-3 sentences as if you're explaining it to a colleague who doesn't work in finance. Use a simple real-world analogy if possible.
💡 Why this works: This is as simple as prompts get — a direct question with a clear constraint. Perfect for beginners to try their first prompt.
🚚 4. Operations / Supply Chain
Supplier Delay Notification
Act as a procurement professional at Harwin, a precision electronics manufacturer. Write a professional email to [SUPPLIER NAME] about a late delivery. Our PO [PO NUMBER] was due on [DATE] and we need the parts for [REASON e.g. a customer order shipping on 10 April]. Request an updated delivery date and ask them to confirm by [DEADLINE]. Tone: firm but maintaining the business relationship.
💡 Why this works: Specifying the tone ("firm but maintaining the business relationship") is one of the most powerful levers in any prompt. ChatGPT can adjust between aggressive and diplomatic — but you have to tell it which.
Process Improvement Brainstorm
You are a lean manufacturing consultant. Suggest 5 practical improvements for the following process at an electronics connector manufacturing facility: [DESCRIBE YOUR CURRENT PROCESS IN 2-3 SENTENCES] For each suggestion, rate the effort as Low/Medium/High and estimate the potential impact as Low/Medium/High. Format as a table with columns: Suggestion, Effort, Impact.
💡 Why this works: Asking for an effort/impact rating turns a brainstorm into a prioritised action list you can take straight to your manager.
Monthly Production Summary
I'm going to paste my production notes from this month. Please organise them into a clean summary with these sections: Completed, In Progress, Delays/Issues, Actions for Next Month. Use bullet points and keep it concise enough for a Monday morning stand-up. [PASTE YOUR NOTES HERE]
💡 Why this works: Defining section headings upfront means ChatGPT organises your messy notes into a presentation-ready format.

Include: a short company intro (2 sentences), key responsibilities (5-6 bullet points), essential requirements, desirable skills, and what we offer. Use welcoming, inclusive language throughout.
💡 Why this works: Listing the exact sections you want acts like a template. ChatGPT fills in each section rather than inventing its own structure.

💡 Why this works: Asking for a "mix" with specific numbers (3 + 3 + 2) ensures variety. The note on what a good answer looks like helps interviewers who aren't sure what to listen for.
Simplify a Policy Document
Rewrite the following company policy section in plain, friendly English that any employee could understand. Keep the meaning exactly the same but remove legal jargon. Add a "Key Takeaway" line at the end summarising it in one sentence. [PASTE POLICY TEXT HERE]
💡 Why this works: "Keep the meaning exactly the same" tells ChatGPT to simplify the language without changing what the policy actually says — an important guardrail.
📊 6. Data & Analytics
Excel Formula Explainer
Explain what this Excel formula does in plain English, step by step. Assume I know the basics of Excel but not advanced functions. Then suggest a simpler alternative if one exists: [PASTE YOUR FORMULA HERE]
💡 Why this works: The simplest prompts often deliver the most value. This saves you from Googling complex nested formulas one function at a time.
SQL Query from Plain English
You are a SQL Server database analyst. I have a table called [TABLE NAME] with these columns: [LIST YOUR COLUMNS e.g. order_id, customer_name, order_date, product_code, quantity, total_value]. Write a SQL query that [DESCRIBE WHAT YOU NEED e.g. "shows total sales by customer for the last 90 days, sorted highest to lowest"]. Add comments explaining each part of the query so I can learn from it.
💡 Why this works: Listing your actual column names means ChatGPT writes a query you can run immediately. The comments turn it into a learning tool.
Dashboard Description for Stakeholders
I've built a Power BI dashboard that shows [DESCRIBE WHAT IT SHOWS e.g. monthly bookings and billings by region]. Write a short description (3-4 sentences) I can put at the top of the dashboard explaining: what it shows, who it's for, and how often it updates. Write for a business audience, not a technical one.
💡 Why this works: Specifying the audience ("business, not technical") controls the language level. A description for directors reads very differently from one for analysts.
✅ 7. Quality / Compliance
Non-Conformance Report Summary
Act as a quality engineer at an electronics connector manufacturer. Write a concise non-conformance report summary using these details: - NCR Number: [NUMBER] - Product: [PART NUMBER AND DESCRIPTION] - Issue found: [DESCRIBE THE DEFECT e.g. plating thickness below minimum specification] - Quantity affected: [NUMBER] out of [BATCH SIZE] - Stage detected: [e.g. incoming inspection, in-process, final test] Include sections for: Description, Root Cause (preliminary), Containment Action, and Recommended Corrective Action. Keep it factual and under 200 words.
💡 Why this works: Feeding in structured data as bullet points and requesting named sections means the output matches your quality management system format.

For each question, suggest a one-line answer or evidence I should have ready. Format as a numbered list.
💡 Why this works: By asking ChatGPT to play the auditor role, you get questions from the other side of the table — invaluable for preparation.
Process Document Simplifier
I'm going to paste a process document. Please rewrite it in simpler language so a new starter could follow it. Keep all the steps but make the instructions clearer. Use numbered steps. Flag any steps that seem ambiguous with a "⚠️ Clarification needed" note. [PASTE YOUR PROCESS DOCUMENT HERE]
💡 Why this works: "Flag any steps that seem ambiguous" turns ChatGPT into a document reviewer as well as a rewriter. You get a cleaner document AND a list of improvements.
💬 8. Customer Service

💡 Why this works: The four-part numbered structure follows best-practice complaint handling. You get a thorough response without having to remember all four elements each time.
FAQ Answer Drafter
Write a clear, friendly FAQ answer for the question: "[CUSTOMER QUESTION e.g. What is the lead time for custom Datamate connectors?]". Keep it under 80 words. End with a call to action like "Contact our sales team for a detailed quote at sales@harwin.com." Important: do NOT make up specific technical numbers — write the structure and I'll fill in the data from our datasheets.
💡 Why this works: "Do NOT make up specific technical numbers" is a safety guardrail — it stops ChatGPT hallucinating data that sounds right but isn't. Always verify technical details.
Escalation Summary for Management
I need to escalate a customer issue to my manager. Summarise the following email chain into a brief escalation note (5-6 sentences). Include: who the customer is, what the issue is, what we've done so far, and what decision is needed from management. [PASTE EMAIL CHAIN HERE]
💡 Why this works: "What decision is needed from management" forces the summary to be action-oriented. Managers love a clear ask at the end.
✅ Do's and Don'ts — Getting Started
Do
✅ Be specific about what you want. "Write a 100-word email to a supplier about a late delivery" beats "Write an email" every single time.
✅ Tell ChatGPT who to be. Starting with "Act as a quality engineer" or "You are a management accountant" focuses the response dramatically.
✅ Ask for a specific format. "Format as a table", "Use bullet points", or "Keep it under 150 words" — the more constraints, the less editing.
✅ Refine, don't restart. If the first answer isn't right, say "Make it more concise" or "Add more detail on the cost impact." You can have a conversation.
✅ Always review the output. ChatGPT is a drafting assistant, not a finished product machine. Your expertise is still the most important ingredient.
Don't
❌ Don't paste confidential data. Never put customer names, pricing, personal details, or sensitive commercial information into ChatGPT. Use [PLACEHOLDERS] and fill in the real details yourself afterwards.
❌ Don't trust numbers or specs blindly. ChatGPT can generate data that sounds right but is completely fabricated. Always check technical details against datasheets and official sources.
❌ Don't write one-word prompts. "Help" or "Email" gives ChatGPT nothing to work with. The effort you put into the prompt directly determines the quality of the output.
❌ Don't give up after one try. The best results come from refining. If the first answer misses the mark, tell ChatGPT what to change. That's how experienced users get great results.
💡 Tips & Tricks
Tip #1: Start with "Act as a..." to instantly improve any prompt. "Act as an experienced procurement manager" produces dramatically better supplier emails than just "Write a supplier email." This works for any role.
Tip #2: End your prompt with "Format your response as..." to control the output structure. Try "...as a table", "...as numbered steps", or "...as a bullet-point summary." This one trick eliminates most reformatting work.
Tip #3: You can paste text in and ask ChatGPT to do things with it. Paste a long email and say "Summarise this in 3 bullet points." Think of it as handing something to a clever assistant and saying "sort this out for me."
Tip #4: If the response is too long, too formal, or not quite right, just tell it. Say "Make this shorter", "Use a warmer tone", or "Focus more on the cost impact." You don't need a new prompt — just keep the conversation going, like you would with a colleague.
🤖 Feature You're Missing: ChatGPT Agent

Most of us use ChatGPT by typing prompts and reading responses. But there's a powerful feature hiding in plain sight that almost nobody at Harwin has discovered yet: Agent mode.

Instead of just answering questions, Agent can actually do things for you — it browses the web, clicks through pages, fills in forms, reads documents, and completes multi-step tasks autonomously. Think of it as handing a task to a virtual assistant who can use a computer.

🌟 Best Use Case: Deep Web Research

Need to find supplier datasheets, compare competitor specs across multiple websites, or pull together information scattered across different pages? Agent will visit each site, extract the relevant data, and compile it into a structured summary — without you copying and pasting between tabs.

Use Agent to visit the websites of [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2], and [COMPETITOR 3]. Find their [PRODUCT TYPE e.g. 2mm-pitch board-to-board connectors] and create a comparison table covering: pin count options, current rating, operating temperature range, mating cycles, and pricing if publicly listed.

How to try it: In any ChatGPT conversation, look for the Agent option in the model picker dropdown (bottom-left). Select it, then describe what you want done as if you were briefing a colleague. Be specific about the websites to visit and the information to collect.

🏭 Real Harwin Use Case
This month: Sales & Marketing

A member of the Sales team had just come back from Embedded World with 40+ business cards to follow up on. Normally, writing personalised follow-up emails takes the best part of a day. Instead, they used this prompt:

Act as a Harwin sales representative. Write a short follow-up email to an engineer I met at Embedded World who was interested in Gecko connectors for a UAV project. Keep it under 100 words, mention our high-vibration testing data, and suggest a 15-minute call this week.

They tweaked the product name and application for each contact, and had all 40 emails drafted in under an hour. The emails still needed a personal touch and a quick review, but ChatGPT handled about 80% of the heavy lifting. That's a full day compressed into a coffee break.
