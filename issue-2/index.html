📈 Sales & Marketing
🤖 ChatGPT Agents
The Lead Researcher
Turn a list of names into a stack of briefing notes
What it does
Give it a list of prospects (names + companies) and the agent researches each one online — finding their LinkedIn role, what their company does, recent news, and likely pain points. It produces a one-page briefing for every prospect, ready for your call or follow-up email.
How to set it up
  1. Open ChatGPT, click the tool menu, and select Agent
  2. Paste the prompt below
  3. Attach a CSV or paste a list with: name, company, job title
  4. Let it run — comes back in 5–10 mins with a clean briefing pack
Starter prompt
Act as a Harwin sales researcher. For each contact below, produce a 1-page briefing including: - Their current role and 2-3 sentences on responsibilities - Their company's main products/markets - Any recent news (last 6 months) — funding, product launches, leadership changes - Likely pain points relevant to high-reliability connectors - 2 conversation starters tailored to them - Suggested Harwin product family (Gecko, Datamate, Kona, Archer) most relevant Output as one briefing per contact, separated by horizontal rules. Contacts: [PASTE YOUR LIST HERE]
⏱ Saves ~3-4 hours per 20-prospect list
⚙️ Engineering / R&D
🤖 ChatGPT Agents
The Standards Watcher
Monitors aerospace & defence standards so you don't have to
What it does
Set up once, and the agent monitors the relevant standards bodies (AS9100, MIL-STD, DEF STAN, IPC) for updates. When a change drops that affects connector design or qualification, you get an email summary explaining what changed, who it affects, and whether Harwin needs to respond.
How to set it up
  1. Open ChatGPT and start a new Scheduled Agent (set to run weekly)
  2. Paste the prompt below — list the standards relevant to your work
  3. Set output to email; tell it to skip the week if nothing's changed
  4. Review the digest each Monday morning over coffee
Starter prompt
Act as an aerospace and defence standards analyst. Once a week, check for updates to the following standards: - [LIST YOUR STANDARDS e.g. AS9100D, MIL-STD-1344, DEF STAN 59-411, IPC-A-610] For each update, produce a short summary including: 1. Standard reference and revision number 2. Date of change 3. Plain-English summary of what changed (3-4 sentences) 4. Whether this affects connector design, qualification testing, or material compliance 5. Suggested action for Harwin (if any) If no relevant changes, simply reply "No relevant standards updates this week." Send results by email at 8am every Monday.
⏱ Saves ~2-3 hours/week of manual standards-checking
💰 Finance / Accounts
🧠 Claude (Excel add-on)
The Variance Spotter
Flags what matters in your month-end pack before you do
What it does
Drop your month-end Excel file into the Claude add-on and the agent scans every line, flags variances over a threshold you set (e.g. ±5% or ±£10k), explains each one in plain English, and drafts your commentary section. You review and tweak — much faster than starting from a blank cell.
How to set it up
  1. Open your month-end file in Excel
  2. Open the Claude for Excel add-on from the ribbon
  3. Paste the prompt below into the chat panel
  4. Claude reads the workbook directly — no copy-paste needed
Starter prompt
Act as a Harwin management accountant. Review the open workbook and produce: 1. A list of all variances above ±5% OR above ±£10,000 (whichever is larger), sorted by absolute value 2. For each, the most likely driver based on the data (FX, volume, mix, one-offs, etc.) 3. A draft commentary paragraph (3-4 sentences) explaining the overall month, suitable for the board pack 4. 3 questions I should ask the budget holder before signing off Use British English. Round figures sensibly. Flag anything that looks like a data entry error rather than a real variance.
⏱ Saves ~half a day at month-end
🏭 Operations / Supply Chain
🤖 ChatGPT Agents
The Supplier Watchdog
Catches supplier risks early — before they bite your production line
What it does
The agent monitors news, financial filings, and industry sources for your key suppliers daily. It alerts you to things that could disrupt supply: factory issues, M&A activity, raw material shortages, financial distress, geographic risks. You get a heads-up days before it shows up as a late delivery.
How to set it up
  1. Open ChatGPT, start a Scheduled Agent (daily at 7am)
  2. List your top 10 critical suppliers
  3. Paste the prompt and let it run for a week
  4. Tune the threshold based on signal-to-noise after week 1
Starter prompt
Act as a supply chain risk analyst at Harwin (a UK precision connector manufacturer). Each weekday morning, scan the news for risks affecting these suppliers: [LIST YOUR TOP 10 SUPPLIERS HERE] Flag any of the following: - Factory fires, strikes, or production stoppages - Major M&A activity or ownership changes - Financial distress (profit warnings, debt issues, layoffs) - Raw material shortages affecting their inputs - Geopolitical risks (sanctions, trade restrictions) - Quality issues or recalls Output as a brief email digest. For each item: supplier, source URL, 2-sentence summary, recommended action (monitor / contact supplier / source alternative). If no risks detected, reply "All quiet on the supplier front today."
⏱ Catches issues 3-5 days earlier than reactive monitoring
👥 HR / People
🧠 Claude
The CV Sifter
First-pass screening that's faster, fairer, and more consistent
What it does
Drop a folder of CVs into Claude with your job spec, and the agent scores each one against your essential and desirable criteria. You get a ranked shortlist with reasoning for each score — and crucially, the agent applies the same criteria to every CV, removing the inconsistency of first-pass human screening on a Friday afternoon.
How to set it up
  1. Open Claude (web or desktop app)
  2. Upload your job description and all candidate CVs
  3. Paste the prompt below, listing your essential and desirable criteria
  4. Review the ranked output — and importantly, do your own review of the top 5
Starter prompt
Act as an HR screening assistant for Harwin. I have uploaded a job description and [N] candidate CVs. For each CV, score against: - Essential criteria: [LIST 3-5 ESSENTIAL REQUIREMENTS] - Desirable criteria: [LIST 3-5 NICE-TO-HAVES] Output as a ranked table: | Rank | Candidate (initials only) | Essential score /5 | Desirable score /5 | Total /10 | Key strengths | Concerns/gaps | Do NOT consider name, age, gender, ethnicity, or anything that could introduce bias. Score only on demonstrated experience and skills mentioned in the CV. Flag any candidate whose CV mentions safeguarding red flags or unexplained gaps over 12 months. This is a first-pass screen only — final shortlist decisions remain with the hiring manager.
⏱ Saves ~2 hours per role; improves consistency
📊 Data & Analytics
🧠 Claude
The Daily KPI Briefing
Pulls the dashboard, spots anomalies, posts to Teams — at 8am sharp
What it does
The agent connects to our database, pulls overnight KPIs (bookings, billings, backlog, OTD), compares to last week and last year, flags anomalies above 2 standard deviations, and posts a tidy summary to a Teams channel before anyone arrives. Senior leaders read it on their phone with their first coffee. No more "where are we today?" questions in your inbox.
How to set it up
  1. In Claude, set up a scheduled task to run weekdays at 7:45am
  2. Connect Claude to the relevant SQL database (read-only)
  3. Paste the prompt below — adjust KPI names to match your tables
  4. Set output to a Teams webhook for your SLT channel
Starter prompt
Act as Harwin's daily KPI analyst. Each weekday at 7:45am, run the following: 1. Query yesterday's totals for: Bookings, Billings, Backlog, OTD%, Open NCRs 2. Compare each to: - Same day last week (% change) - Same day last year (% change) - Month-to-date vs forecast 3. Flag any metric outside ±2 standard deviations of its trailing 30-day average 4. Identify the top 3 customers/products driving the biggest movement Format the output for Microsoft Teams (use markdown headings, bullets, and emoji indicators 🟢🟡🔴 for green/amber/red). Keep it under 250 words — readable on a phone in 60 seconds. If a metric can't be calculated (data missing, system down), say so explicitly rather than skipping it.
⏱ Saves ~45 mins/day; SLT gets visibility 2 hours earlier
✅ Quality / Compliance
⚙️ Cowork
The NCR Pattern Finder
Reads every NCR, spots the recurring stories you can't see line-by-line
What it does
Point Cowork at the folder where NCRs are stored, and the agent reads every one, extracts structured data (defect type, product family, root cause, customer impact), and produces a monthly trend report. The patterns that emerge — "we keep seeing the same plating issue from one supplier" — are the ones that drive real CAPA wins.
How to set it up
  1. Open Cowork on your desktop
  2. Point it at the NCR folder (typically a SharePoint or shared drive location)
  3. Paste the prompt below
  4. Schedule it to run on the 1st of each month and email you the report
Starter prompt
Act as Harwin's quality data analyst. Read every NCR in the specified folder from the last 30 days. For each NCR, extract: - NCR number and date - Product family (Gecko, Datamate, Kona, Archer, other) - Defect category (mechanical, electrical, plating/finish, packaging, documentation) - Stage detected (incoming, in-process, final test, customer) - Quantity affected - Suspected root cause (if recorded) - Supplier involved (if applicable) Output a monthly trend report with: 1. Total NCRs vs previous month and 12-month average 2. Top 5 defect categories by frequency and by quantity 3. Any defect-supplier or defect-product combination appearing 3+ times 4. New defect types not seen in the previous quarter 5. Three suggested CAPA priorities based on the patterns Save the report to /Quality/Monthly Trends/ as a PDF.
⏱ Saves ~1 day/month; surfaces patterns humans miss
💬 Customer Service
⚙️ Cowork
The Inbox Triage Agent
Sorts the urgent from the not-so-urgent before you even open Outlook
What it does
Cowork reads new messages in your shared customer inbox, categorises them by urgency and type, drafts a first-pass response for routine queries, and flags anything urgent for immediate attention. You walk in to a sorted, prioritised inbox with draft replies waiting for review — not a pile of 80 unread.
How to set it up
  1. In Cowork, connect your shared customer service mailbox (Outlook)
  2. Paste the prompt below
  3. Set it to run every 30 mins during business hours
  4. For week 1, review every draft before sending — tune as needed
Starter prompt
Act as a customer service triage assistant for Harwin. For each new email in the shared customer service mailbox: 1. Categorise by type: Order status / Technical query / Quality complaint / Pricing or quote / Returns/RMA / Other 2. Categorise by urgency: 🔴 Urgent (customer line down, escalation, complaint) / 🟡 Standard (response needed today) / 🟢 Low (FYI, acknowledged) 3. For Standard and Low priority, draft a reply using Harwin's tone (professional, empathetic, helpful) with placeholders [LIKE THIS] for any specific data I need to check 4. For Urgent, do NOT draft a reply — flag with a Teams notification to the on-duty rep 5. Move emails into Outlook folders matching the type categories NEVER auto-send. All drafts await human review. Do NOT make up technical specifications, pricing, or delivery commitments. If a query asks for those, the draft should say "checking with [team] and will respond by [time]" instead.
⏱ Saves ~1.5 hours/day; nothing urgent slips through the cracks
🆕 What's New This Month
ChatGPT Extended Thinking Mode Game Changer
ChatGPT has a new mode that takes longer to respond but produces noticeably better results. It now mimics Claude's style — showing its thinking, considering options, and refining the answer before giving it to you. Particularly good for legal documents, complex analysis, and anything that needs care and accuracy.
How to find it: Click your name (bottom-left) → Settings → Personalisation. Switch to Extended mode. Yes, it's slower — but the quality difference is significant for important work.
Daily Notifications from ChatGPT Useful
ChatGPT can now send you scheduled daily notifications — gold prices, share prices, market data, currency rates, anything you'd otherwise have to check manually. Treat it like a partner that updates you proactively rather than waiting to be asked.
Try it: Tell ChatGPT something like "Send me the FTSE 100 closing price every weekday at 5pm" and follow its setup prompts. Great for anyone tracking metrics that change daily.
Claude Now Builds PowerPoint Decks New
Claude has native PowerPoint integration. Hand it your content, give it some direction on style, and it produces a finished deck you can edit. Faster than starting from a blank slide, and the formatting is genuinely good.
Claude Live Dashboards Big News
Connect Claude to a database, and it'll produce a live dashboard that refreshes 24/7 — sitting on a webpage just like our existing KPI dashboards, but built entirely through conversation. This has the potential to be a real alternative to Power BI for some use cases. Worth keeping an eye on.
"Vibe Coding" — Live Python in Spreadsheets For the Curious
One of our colleagues has been using AI to write Python live inside Excel — describing what they want in plain English and watching the AI produce working code right there in the spreadsheet. You don't need a development background; the AI handles the syntax. A good reminder that "code" doesn't have to mean "for developers" anymore.

💡 Tips & Tricks for Running Agents
Tip #1: Start small and supervised. For the first week of any agent, review every output before acting on it. You'll catch quirks early and can refine the prompt before the agent takes on real responsibility.
Tip #2: Always include a "do NOT" instruction. Agents are eager to help — sometimes too eager. Tell yours what it must NOT do (e.g. "do not auto-send emails", "do not invent figures", "do not commit changes without my approval").
Tip #3: Schedule wisely. Agents that run too often create noise; too rarely and they miss things. Daily for monitoring, weekly for digests, on-demand for one-off jobs is a good rule of thumb.
Tip #4: Treat agents as colleagues, not magic. The output is only as good as the brief. If you'd struggle to explain the task to a new starter on day one, the agent will struggle too. Be specific about what "good" looks like.

Approved AI Tools at Harwin — and Why It Matters

All eight agents above use one of three approved AI tools. Sticking to these protects you and the business. Don't run agents on personal accounts — agents handle data autonomously and unsupervised, so security matters more than ever.
✓ ChatGPT ✓ Microsoft Copilot ✓ Claude
The golden rule: always use your Harwin enterprise account, never a personal one — especially for agents.
  • Enterprise tenants are ring-fenced — Harwin data isn't used to train external models
  • Personal accounts aren't — and agents process much more data than chat sessions
  • Already on a personal account? The Data & Analytics team can help you migrate
  • Need a specialist tool (e.g. Cursor, Perplexity, niche AI products)? Email IT to get a subscription — we're unlikely to say no, but want visibility and proper licensing rather than tools appearing on personal credit cards
The three approved tools cover 95% of what most teams need. For anything else, just email IT to request a subscription — easier than expensing it later.
